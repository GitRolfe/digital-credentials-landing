What Are Digital Credentials?

Digital credentials are secure, digitally signed identity or entitlement documents stored in a digital wallet. They can be issued by governments, businesses, or organizations.

Three Ways to Use Digital Credentials

1

One-off checks

(no user account needed)

Verify a digital credential without tying it to a user profile. Check age, licence validity, or entitlement. Perfect for online age gates, simple compliance checks, and lightweight verification flows.

2

Identify & recover accounts

(with biometric assurance)

Use digital credentials + a live biometric match to securely identify a user. Map credential claims to a user and require face matching to ensure the presenter is the real owner. Ideal for account recovery.

3

Strong authentication factor

(like MFA)

Treat a digital credential + biometric match as a high-assurance MFA factor. Works similarly to passkeys but with verified identity claims. Ideal for sensitive transactions and secure access flows.
